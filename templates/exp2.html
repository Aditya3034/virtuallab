Experiment 2 - Binary Amplitude Shift Keying

Aim Apparatus Theory Procedure Conclusion Simulation

Aim: -

To Observe Modulation and Demodulation of Binary Amplitude Shift Keying


Apparatus: -

Data Conditioning and Carrier Modulation Transmitter Trainer Kit, CRO,Patchcords.


Theory: -

Carrier modulation is a technique by which digital data is made to modulate a continuous wave carrier. For all type of carrier modulation the carrier frequency should be at least 2 times that of modulating frequency to satisfy the Nyquist criterion (sampling Theorem).



Amplitude Shift Keying Modulation Techniques :

In amplitude shift keying , the carrier is transmitted when the modulationdata is ‘one’ and the carrier is rejected from transmission when the data is ‘zero’. The ASK modolators employs an Analog Multiplexer as a modulating Switch,which can switch between carrier and ground, for every 1 and O transitions.The carrier frequency chosen for ASK modulation is 2MHz.


Procedure: -

  1. Connect AC supply to the kit.
  2. Make connections as per the Circuit Diagram.
  3. Switch on the power
  4. Observe the following waveforms on CRO:

    I. Input data

    II. Carrier Frequency

    III. Modulated signal at Modulated Output.


Conclusion: -

Thus Amplitude-shift keying (ASK) is a form of amplitude modulation that represents digital data as variations in the amplitude of a carrier wave. In an ASK system, a symbol, representing one or more bits, is sent by transmitting a fixed-amplitude carrier wave at a fixed frequency for a specific time duration.
